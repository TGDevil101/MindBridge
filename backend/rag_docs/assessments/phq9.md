# PHQ-9 — Patient Health Questionnaire 9-item

## What it is
The PHQ-9 is a 9-question screening tool developed by Kroenke, Spitzer, and Williams (2001) for assessing the presence and severity of depressive symptoms. It is the most widely used brief depression screen in primary care globally.

## What it measures
Over the past two weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following?

1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things
2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless
3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much
4. Feeling tired or having little energy
5. Poor appetite or overeating
6. Feeling bad about yourself, or that you are a failure
7. Trouble concentrating
8. Moving or speaking slowly, or being fidgety/restless
9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way

Each item is rated 0–3:
- 0 = Not at all
- 1 = Several days
- 2 = More than half the days
- 3 = Nearly every day

## Scoring bands
Total score is 0–27. The bands are:

- **0–4 Minimal depression.** Symptoms are within the normal range.
- **5–9 Mild depression.** Symptoms present and worth monitoring.
- **10–14 Moderate depression.** Professional evaluation recommended.
- **15–19 Moderately severe depression.** Active treatment likely indicated.
- **20–27 Severe depression.** Prompt clinical attention strongly recommended.

## CRITICAL: Question 9 (suicide/self-harm item)
**Any non-zero response on item 9 — "thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself" — triggers crisis protocol regardless of the total score.**

This means: a person scoring 8 overall (mild range) BUT with item 9 = 1 ("several days") must be treated as a crisis case. The helpline must be surfaced. This is a non-negotiable rule because item 9 directly screens for suicidal ideation, which carries acute risk even when overall depressive severity is low.

## Important framing rules
- The PHQ-9 is a **screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument**.
- Never say "you have depression." Use: "your responses are associated with [moderate/severe] depressive symptoms, which is worth professional evaluation."
- A high score is not a verdict — it is a signal worth taking seriously.

## Indian context
- Validated in Indian populations including student samples (Indu et al., 2018, Patel et al., 2008).
- In urban India, item 9 endorsement is more common among adolescents in academic-pressure environments. Always take seriously.
