# GAD-7 — Generalised Anxiety Disorder 7-item scale

## What it is
The GAD-7 is a 7-question screening tool developed by Spitzer et al. (2006) for identifying probable generalised anxiety disorder. It is the most widely used brief anxiety screen in primary care and adolescent mental health settings worldwide.

## What it measures
Over the past two weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following?

1. Feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge
2. Not being able to stop or control worrying
3. Worrying too much about different things
4. Trouble relaxing
5. Being so restless that it is hard to sit still
6. Becoming easily annoyed or irritable
7. Feeling afraid as if something awful might happen

Each item is rated 0–3:
- 0 = Not at all
- 1 = Several days
- 2 = More than half the days
- 3 = Nearly every day

## Scoring bands
Total score is 0–21. The bands are:

- **0–4 Minimal anxiety.** Symptoms are within the normal range. Continued self-monitoring may be helpful but no clinical action is typically needed.
- **5–9 Mild anxiety.** Symptoms present but generally manageable. Self-care strategies (sleep, exercise, journaling, breathing) are appropriate. Worth monitoring; not yet at threshold for professional referral unless distress is high.
- **10–14 Moderate anxiety.** Suggests a likely anxiety disorder warranting professional evaluation. A counsellor or psychologist is recommended.
- **15–21 Severe anxiety.** Strongly suggests significant impairment. Prompt professional consultation is recommended.

## Important framing rules
- The GAD-7 is a **screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument**. Only a qualified clinician can diagnose generalised anxiety disorder.
- A score in any band does not equal a diagnosis. The score reflects what someone reported about the past two weeks.
- The language MindBridge should use: "your score is X, which falls in the [band] range, and means some of your responses are associated with [mild/moderate/severe] anxiety symptoms. This is a screen, not a diagnosis."
- Never say "you have anxiety." Use deferred language: "your responses suggest you may be experiencing..."

## Indian context
- GAD-7 has been validated in Indian student and adult populations (Bhattacharyya et al., 2018).
- Cultural note: Indian respondents sometimes under-report anxiety symptoms due to stigma. A "mild" score from someone clearly distressed warrants follow-up regardless.
