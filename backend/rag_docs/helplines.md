# Mental health helplines in India

## iCall (TISS, Mumbai)
- Phone: 9152987821
- Hours: Monday to Saturday, 8 AM to 10 PM
- Cost: Free
- Languages: English, Hindi, Marathi, Gujarati, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Bengali, Punjabi
- Service: Telephone and email-based counselling provided by trained mental health professionals
- Email: icall@tiss.edu
- For: Anyone over the age of 13 experiencing emotional or psychological distress
- Confidential: Yes
- Web: https://icallhelpline.org

## Vandrevala Foundation
- Phone: 1860-2662-345 (also 1800-2333-330)
- Hours: 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
- Cost: Free
- Languages: English, Hindi, and most major Indian languages
- Service: Crisis intervention, suicide prevention, mental health counselling
- WhatsApp: +91 9999666555
- For: Anyone needing immediate emotional support
- Confidential: Yes

## Emergency services
- India national emergency: 112
- For situations of immediate danger to self or others (self-harm in progress, suicide attempt, medical emergency)
- Available 24/7

## When to use which helpline
- Immediate physical danger: call 112 first, then iCall or Vandrevala for follow-up
- Active suicidal thoughts or in significant distress: Vandrevala (24/7 availability)
- Need to talk to a counsellor during daytime: iCall (more counsellor-led, structured)
- Outside iCall hours and need someone to talk to: Vandrevala
- Anonymous text-based support: Vandrevala WhatsApp +91 9999666555

## Important notes
- All listed helplines are free and confidential
- None of these helplines store personally identifying information unless the caller explicitly shares it
- Speaking to a school counsellor is also a valid first step for students whose schools provide counselling services
